# Benchmarks — Vertical B2B SaaS by Stage

Industry benchmarks for vertical B2B SaaS companies, segmented by stage.
Vertical SaaS typically has higher NRR/GRR and lower CAC than horizontal SaaS
due to deeper workflow integration and concentrated buyer communities.

## Metrics by Stage

| Metric | Target | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| MRR Growth | >15% MoM | Measured after first 10 customers |
| Logo Churn | <3% monthly | Acceptable to be higher pre-PMF |
| ACV | $5K–50K | Vertical SaaS tends toward higher ACV |
| Customers | 5–30 | Quality > quantity at this stage |
| CAC Payback | <18 months | Often founder-led sales; keep tracking |
| Gross Margin | >65% | Services component OK during setup |

**What matters most at this stage:** Evidence of pull (inbound demand, repeat usage,
referrals) matters more than any single metric. The founder's ability to get meetings
with target buyers on the strength of the idea alone is a strong signal.

## ARR Multiple Ranges

Valuation multiples for vertical B2B SaaS vary by market conditions, growth rate,
and profitability. These ranges reflect 2024-2026 market conditions.

### By Growth Rate (EV/NTM Revenue)

| ARR Growth YoY | Typical Multiple | Premium Multiple |
|----------------|------------------|------------------|
| >80% | 15–25x | 30x+ (rare) |
| 50–80% | 10–18x | 20x+ |
| 30–50% | 7–12x | 15x |
| 15–30% | 5–8x | 10x |
| <15% | 3–6x | 7x |

### By Stage (Private Rounds)

| Stage | Typical ARR Multiple | Notes |
|-------|---------------------|-------|
| Seed | N/A (valued on team + TAM) | Pre-revenue; $5-15M post-money typical |
| Series A | 20-40x ARR | If $1-3M ARR; drops as ARR rises |
| Series B | 15-25x ARR | If $5-15M ARR |
| Series C+ | 10-20x ARR | If $20M+ ARR; efficiency matters more |
| Growth / Pre-IPO | 8-15x ARR | IPO-readiness premium |

### Vertical SaaS Premium/Discount

Vertical SaaS companies sometimes trade at a **discount** to horizontal SaaS peers
due to perceived TAM constraints. However, top-performing vertical SaaS can command
a **premium** because:

- Higher NRR (deeper workflow embedding)
- Lower CAC (concentrated buyer community, word-of-mouth)
- More predictable revenue (higher switching costs)
- Stronger path to profitability (efficient GTM)
- PE acquisition appetite (predictable cash flow machines)

The discount typically applies at IPO (public market investors prefer large TAM
narratives) while the premium applies in PE-led transactions.

## Vertical-Specific Considerations

Different verticals have structural characteristics that affect benchmark
expectations. Adjust scoring accordingly.

### Healthcare / Life Sciences
- **Regulatory weight:** HIPAA, FDA compliance create high barriers but slow sales
- **Typical ACV:** $50K–500K+ (enterprise-heavy)
- **Sales cycle:** 6-18 months (compliance review adds time)
- **Benchmark adjustment:** Accept longer CAC payback (up to 24 months) due to
  higher ACV and stickier contracts

### Construction / Trades
- **Buyer sophistication:** Lower technology adoption; UI/UX is a differentiator
- **Typical ACV:** $5K–50K (SMB to mid-market)
- **Sales cycle:** 2-6 months
- **Benchmark adjustment:** Logo churn may be higher (SMB mortality); compensate
  with high expansion within surviving accounts

### Financial Services / FinTech
- **Regulatory weight:** Heavy (banking, insurance, securities regulations)
- **Typical ACV:** $20K–200K+
- **Sales cycle:** 6-12 months (security review + compliance)
- **Benchmark adjustment:** Higher gross margin expected (>80%); payment/transaction
  revenue can supplement SaaS

### Legal
- **Buyer behavior:** Conservative; peer referrals are critical GTM
- **Typical ACV:** $10K–100K
- **Sales cycle:** 3-9 months
- **Benchmark adjustment:** Word-of-mouth CAC can be very low; NRR driven by
  firm growth (seat expansion)

### Real Estate / PropTech
- **Market cyclicality:** Revenue can be affected by real estate cycles
- **Typical ACV:** $5K–50K (varies wildly by segment)
- **Sales cycle:** 2-6 months
- **Benchmark adjustment:** Factor cyclicality into growth projections; GRR may
  dip in downturns even if product is strong

### Agriculture / AgTech
- **Buyer behavior:** Seasonal purchasing; long decision cycles
- **Typical ACV:** $5K–30K
- **Sales cycle:** 3-12 months (aligned with growing seasons)
- **Benchmark adjustment:** MRR may be lumpy (seasonal); annual metrics more
  reliable than monthly

### Logistics / Supply Chain
- **Integration complexity:** Deep ERP and hardware integrations required
- **Typical ACV:** $20K–200K
- **Sales cycle:** 6-12 months
- **Benchmark adjustment:** Higher implementation costs (lower initial gross margin)
  that improve over time as services phase out

### Education / EdTech
- **Buyer behavior:** Budgets cycle annually; procurement is slow
- **Typical ACV:** $5K–100K (K-12 vs Higher Ed varies widely)
- **Sales cycle:** 3-12 months (aligned with academic/fiscal year)
- **Benchmark adjustment:** Revenue is seasonal; renewal rates are high once adopted
  (curriculum integration creates switching cost)
