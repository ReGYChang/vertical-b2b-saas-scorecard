# PMF Stages — Vertical B2B SaaS

Product-Market Fit is not binary; it's a spectrum. This framework defines four stages
with specific evidence thresholds for each. The stages help founders understand where
they are and what must be true to advance.

## Stage Overview

| Stage | Typical ARR | Signal Summary | Funding Stage |
|-------|-------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Pre-PMF** | $0–300K | Searching; hypothesis-driven | Pre-seed / Seed |
| **Emerging PMF** | $300K–2M | First repeatable patterns | Seed / Series A |
| **Strong PMF** | $2M–10M | Clear pull; retention proven | Series A / B |
| **Scaling PMF** | $10M+ | Machine works; optimize | Series B+ |

## Stage 1: Pre-PMF

**Definition:** You are searching for PMF. You have hypotheses about who will buy,
why they will buy, and how much they will pay — but limited evidence.

### Evidence Checklist (need 3+ to claim Pre-PMF)

- [ ] Completed 30+ customer discovery interviews in the target vertical
- [ ] Identified a specific, named pain point that 70%+ of interviewees confirm
- [ ] Have a working prototype or MVP that at least 5 target users have tried
- [ ] Can articulate the buyer persona (title, budget, decision process)
- [ ] Have at least 1 paying customer (even at a discounted rate)
- [ ] Sales conversations do not require "educating" the buyer about the problem

### Key Questions to Answer

1. **Is this a hair-on-fire problem?** Would the buyer's day measurably worsen
   without a solution, or is this a nice-to-have optimization?
2. **Who writes the check?** Can you name the specific title and department that
   has budget authority?
3. **What is the current workaround?** How are they solving this today
   (spreadsheets, manual process, legacy software, outsourcing)?
4. **What triggers the purchase?** What event makes the buyer decide to look for
   a solution (audit, regulation change, growth pain, competitor pressure)?

### Advancement Criteria → Emerging PMF

All of the following must be true:
- 5+ paying customers acquired without extreme discounting (>50% of target ACV)
- At least 2 customers came from outside the founder's immediate network
- First cohort shows weekly product usage for 3+ consecutive months
- You can describe a repeatable ICP (not "anyone who will pay")

### Common Failure Modes

- **Solution looking for a problem:** Building features before validating pain
- **Founder's network masquerading as demand:** First 5 customers are all friends
- **Vitamin vs painkiller confusion:** Buyers say "interesting" but don't buy
- **Over-customization:** Every customer needs a different version of the product
- **Wrong buyer level:** Talking to users who love it but don't have budget

## Stage 2: Emerging PMF

**Definition:** You have early evidence of repeatable demand. Some customers are
genuinely retained and happy, but the signal is inconsistent — some cohorts retain
well, others don't. The sales motion is forming but not yet predictable.

### Evidence Checklist (need 5+ to claim Emerging PMF)

- [ ] 10-50 paying customers
- [ ] 3-month retention rate > 80% for best cohort
- [ ] At least 20% of customers came from organic/referral channels
- [ ] Can describe a clear ICP that represents >60% of customer base
- [ ] ACV is stabilizing (not wildly different per customer)
- [ ] At least 3 customers have expanded (seats, modules, usage tiers)
- [ ] Sales cycle length is becoming predictable (±30% variance)
- [ ] Customer support tickets are about "how to do more" not "this is broken"
- [ ] NPS > 30 from a survey of 20+ customers

### Key Questions to Answer

1. **Which cohort retains best and why?** Segment by industry sub-vertical, company
   size, use case, and acquisition channel. Find the "magic" segment.
2. **Is the sales motion repeatable?** Could a new hire replicate founder-led sales
   with a playbook?
3. **What drives expansion?** Is it organic (users discover more features) or
   CSM-driven (you have to sell them on upgrading)?
4. **What causes churn?** Categorize: product gaps, wrong ICP, pricing, champion
   departure, or company closure?

### Advancement Criteria → Strong PMF

All of the following must be true:
- Quarterly NRR > 100% for 2+ consecutive quarters
- Logo retention > 85% annually (or projected from monthly data)
- A non-founder sales rep has closed 5+ deals independently
- >30% of pipeline comes from inbound or referral
- Can predict quarterly bookings within ±20%

### Common Failure Modes

- **Premature scaling:** Hiring sales team before motion is repeatable
- **ICP drift:** Accepting customers outside the sweet spot to hit revenue targets
- **Metrics theater:** Reporting ARR but hiding heavy discounts, multi-year prepays,
  or services revenue counted as subscription
- **Champion dependency:** Deals close because of 1 internal champion who leaves
- **Feature bloat:** Adding features for edge-case customers instead of deepening
  core value for the ICP

## Stage 3: Strong PMF

**Definition:** PMF is evident and repeatable. The core ICP is clearly defined,
retention is proven across multiple cohorts, and the sales motion works beyond
the founding team. The challenge shifts from "does this work?" to "how fast can
we scale without breaking it?"

### Evidence Checklist (need 6+ to claim Strong PMF)

- [ ] 50-200+ paying customers
- [ ] NRR > 110% for 3+ consecutive quarters
- [ ] Logo retention > 88% annually
- [ ] 40%+ of new business from inbound/referral/organic
- [ ] Multiple sales reps succeeding independently (not just the founder)
- [ ] Clear expansion playbook (upsell paths defined and working)
- [ ] Gross margin > 70% and trending upward
- [ ] Customer cohort analysis shows consistent retention across vintages
- [ ] Win rate in competitive deals > 40%
- [ ] Product used daily or weekly by end users (not just admin/buyer)

### Key Questions to Answer

1. **What's the ceiling on the current ICP?** How many more customers fit the exact
   profile you're selling to today?
2. **Where does the second product come from?** Is there a natural expansion into
   adjacent workflow, persona, or sub-vertical?
3. **Can you raise prices?** Have you tested pricing power? Vertical SaaS with
   strong PMF often underprices.
4. **What breaks at 2x the team?** Identify the operational bottlenecks
   (onboarding, support, engineering) that would fail at double the scale.

### Advancement Criteria → Scaling PMF

All of the following must be true:
- Proven multi-product or multi-segment expansion (second product contributing >10% of new ARR)
- Sales efficiency (Magic Number > 0.8 or CAC payback < 15 months)
- Organization can operate without founder in daily sales/CS
- Rule of 40 > 40%
- Board-level agreement on the path to $100M ARR

### Common Failure Modes

- **Scaling before systemizing:** Hiring aggressively without documented processes
- **Ignoring SMB churn to chase enterprise:** Enterprise deals are larger but
  slower; SMB base erodes while you're distracted
- **Underinvesting in platform:** Product becomes fragile under scale because
  engineering time went to features not infrastructure
- **Founder bottleneck:** CEO still involved in every deal, hire, and feature decision

## Stage 4: Scaling PMF

**Definition:** The growth machine is working. PMF is not just proven but systematized.
The organization can predictably turn capital into revenue growth. The focus shifts
to efficiency, market expansion, and building a durable competitive position.

### Evidence Checklist (need 7+ to claim Scaling PMF)

- [ ] $10M+ ARR
- [ ] NRR > 115% sustained
- [ ] Logo retention > 90% annually
- [ ] Rule of 40 > 40%
- [ ] Multiple products contributing meaningful revenue
- [ ] Sales team operating with documented playbook and consistent quota attainment
- [ ] Brand is recognized in the vertical (conference keynotes, analyst coverage)
- [ ] International or adjacent-vertical expansion underway or planned
- [ ] Burn multiple < 2x (or FCF positive)
- [ ] Can model the path to $100M ARR with reasonable assumptions

### Key Questions at This Stage

1. **Is this a platform or a product?** Platform companies (Veeva, Toast, ServiceTitan)
   build ecosystems; product companies get acquired. Which path are you on?
2. **What's the exit strategy?** IPO, strategic acquisition, or PE? Each requires
   different preparation.
3. **Where are the margin expansion opportunities?** Payments, fintech, data products,
   marketplace — what can layer on top of the core SaaS?
4. **What would it take to lose PMF?** Regulation change, platform shift, AI disruption,
   horizontal competitor entry — stress-test the thesis.

## PMF Diagnostics

When the PMF stage is unclear, use these diagnostic questions to determine where
a company sits. Each question maps to a specific stage boundary.

### Pre-PMF → Emerging PMF Boundary

| Diagnostic | Pre-PMF Answer | Emerging PMF Answer |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| How do you get customers? | "I personally know all of them" | "Some find us on their own" |
| Can you predict next quarter's revenue? | "No idea" | "Within 2x range" |
| Do customers look similar? | "Every customer is unique" | "A pattern is forming" |
| What's your churn reason? | "We don't know / too early" | "We can categorize top 3 reasons" |

### Emerging PMF → Strong PMF Boundary

| Diagnostic | Emerging PMF Answer | Strong PMF Answer |
|-----------|-------------------|------------------|
| Can a new sales hire succeed? | "Probably not yet" | "Yes, we have a playbook" |
| Is NRR > 100% consistently? | "Some quarters yes, some no" | "Every quarter for 3+ quarters" |
| What % is inbound? | "<30%" | ">30-40%" |
| Do cohorts retain consistently? | "Best cohorts do, others vary" | "All ICP-fit cohorts retain well" |

### Strong PMF → Scaling PMF Boundary

| Diagnostic | Strong PMF Answer | Scaling PMF Answer |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------|
| Can you grow without the founder selling? | "Getting there" | "Yes, team operates independently" |
| Is the second product working? | "Launched but early" | "Contributing >10% of new ARR" |
| Rule of 40? | "Close to 40%" | "Consistently above 40%" |
| Can you model $100M ARR path? | "Aspirational" | "Yes, with concrete assumptions" |

## Vertical-Specific PMF Patterns

PMF manifests differently in different verticals. These patterns help calibrate
expectations for the PMF stage assessment.

### "Regulatory trigger" verticals (Healthcare, FinTech, Legal)
PMF often arrives suddenly when a regulatory change creates urgency. Pre-regulatory-trigger,
adoption is slow and voluntary. Post-trigger, it becomes mandatory. Time your entry to
the regulatory cycle. PMF in these verticals looks like: long sales cycle → sudden
acceleration → rapid adoption within a compliance deadline.

### "Word-of-mouth" verticals (Legal, Trades, Real Estate)
In tight-knit professional communities, PMF shows up as conference-driven growth
and peer referrals. A founder who speaks at 3 industry events and gets inbound leads
from each has found PMF signals. These verticals reward "one of us" positioning.

### "Network-effect" verticals (Marketplaces, Supply Chain, Real Estate)
PMF requires reaching a critical mass threshold on both sides of the network. Signs:
before threshold — slow, painful, "chicken and egg." After threshold — exponential
growth, demand outpacing supply. NRR explodes once the network is liquid.

### "Workflow replacement" verticals (Construction, Manufacturing, Agriculture)
PMF in these verticals means replacing deeply embedded manual processes. The adoption
curve is slow (training, change management) but retention is extremely high once
embedded. Early churn may be high (implementation failure) while long-term retention
is excellent. Evaluate 12-month retention, not 3-month.

### "Platform expansion" verticals (Restaurants, Home Services, Fitness)
PMF starts with a wedge product (POS, scheduling, booking) and expands into payments,
lending, payroll, marketing. The wedge may have thin margins; the expansion products
drive LTV and NRR. Evaluate the full platform potential, not just the wedge metrics.
